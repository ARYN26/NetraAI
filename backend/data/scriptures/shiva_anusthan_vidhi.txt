# Shiva Anusthan Vidhi - Complete Sadhana Procedures
Source: Traditional Shaiva Texts and Shiva Purana

## What is Anusthan?

Anusthan (अनुष्ठान) is an intensive spiritual practice undertaken for a specific duration with fixed rules and disciplines. It involves concentrated worship, mantra japa, and observance of specific vows to achieve spiritual goals, fulfill wishes, or deepen devotion.

The word comes from "anu" (following) + "sthā" (to stand/remain) = remaining steadfast in practice.

## Types of Shiva Anusthan

### 1. Laghu Anusthan (Minor Anusthan)
- Duration: 3 days
- Japa count: 18,000 mantras (6,000 per day)
- Suitable for beginners

### 2. Madhyam Anusthan (Medium Anusthan)
- Duration: 7 days (Saptāha)
- Japa count: 42,000 mantras (6,000 per day)
- Recommended for regular practitioners

### 3. Uttam Anusthan (Superior Anusthan)
- Duration: 9 days (Navāha)
- Japa count: 54,000 mantras (6,000 per day)
- For serious seekers

### 4. Ekādaśī Anusthan (11-Day Anusthan)
- Duration: 11 days
- Japa count: 1,25,000 mantras (approximately 11,364 per day)
- Most powerful and traditionally recommended
- This is the standard Shiva Anusthan

### 5. Mahā Anusthan (Great Anusthan)
- Duration: 21 days or 40 days (Maṇḍala)
- Japa count: 1,25,000 to 5,00,000 mantras
- For advanced practitioners only

## 11-Day (Ekādaśī) Shiva Anusthan - Complete Procedure

### Before Beginning (Pūrva Taiyārī)

**1. Select Auspicious Time:**
- Begin on Monday (Somavāra)
- Or on Śrāvaṇa Māsa (July-August)
- Or during Mahāśivarātri period
- Avoid starting during Rāhu Kāla or inauspicious muhurtas
- Best to consult a panchang (almanac)

**2. Prepare Sacred Space:**
- Clean a room or corner for exclusive use during anusthan
- Place a clean āsana (seat) facing North or East
- Install Shiva Lingam or image (if not available, use Bana Lingam or Rudrākṣa)
- Keep all puja items ready
- Remove worldly distractions

**3. Items Required:**
- Shiva Lingam or image
- Rudrākṣa mālā (108 beads) for japa
- Pañcāmṛta ingredients (milk, curd, honey, ghee, sugar)
- Bilva leaves (minimum 11 per day)
- Incense, lamp, camphor
- White flowers
- Sacred ash (vibhūti)
- Pure water in copper vessel
- White cloth for Lingam
- Clean white or light-colored clothes for yourself

### Sankalpa (Intention Setting)

On the first day, before beginning, take this formal vow:

"Om Viṣṇur Viṣṇur Viṣṇuḥ |
Śrīmad Bhagavato Mahā Puruṣasya Viṣṇor Ājñayā Pravartamānasya |
Adya Brahmaṇaḥ Dvitīya Parārdhe Śrī Śvetavarāha Kalpe |
Vaivasvata Manvantare Aṣṭāviṃśati-tame Kaliyuge |
Prathame Pāde [Your Location] Deśe |
[Month] Māse [Paksha] Pakṣe [Tithi] Tithau [Day] Vāsare |
[Your Gotra] Gotrasya [Your Name] Ahaṃ |
Mama Samasta Pāpa-kṣaya Pūrvakam |
Śrīman Mahādeva Prītyartham / [Or specific purpose] |
Ekādaśa Dina Paryantaṃ Mahādeva Anushṭhānaṃ |
Sava Lakṣa Japa Sankhyākam |
Ahaṃ Kariṣye |"

(Translation: Today, I [Name], of [Gotra] gotra, for the pleasure of Lord Mahādeva [or specific purpose], hereby resolve to perform an 11-day anusthan with 1.25 lakh mantra japa.)

### Daily Schedule

**Brahma Muhurta (4:00 - 6:00 AM):**
1. Wake up before sunrise
2. Attend to natural calls
3. Take bath (cold water preferred, or at least head bath)
4. Wear clean white/light clothes

**Morning Worship (6:00 - 9:00 AM):**
1. Light lamp and incense
2. Perform Ācamana
3. Do Prāṇāyāma (3-7 rounds)
4. Invoke Lord Gaṇeśa: "Om Gaṃ Gaṇapataye Namaḥ" (11 times)
5. Invoke Guru: "Om Gurave Namaḥ"
6. Begin Shiva worship with Ṣoḍaśopacāra Pūjā
7. Perform Rudrābhiṣeka with Pañcāmṛta
8. Offer Bilva leaves while chanting mantra
9. Complete morning japa quota

**Mantra for Japa:**
Primary: "Om Namaḥ Śivāya" (Pañcākṣara Mantra)

Or with extended version:
"Om Hrīṃ Namaḥ Śivāya" (Ṣaḍākṣara)
"Om Hauṃ Jūṃ Saḥ" (Mṛtyuñjaya Bīja)

Advanced practitioners may use:
"Om Tryambakaṃ Yajāmahe Sugandhiṃ Puṣṭivardhanam |
Urvārukam-iva Bandhanān Mṛtyor Mukṣīya Māmṛtāt ||"
(Mahāmṛtyuñjaya Mantra)

**Japa Method:**
- Sit facing North or East
- Hold Rudrākṣa mālā in right hand
- Move beads with thumb and middle finger (not index finger)
- One complete mālā = 108 mantras
- Complete minimum 12 mālās morning (1,296 mantras)
- Do not cross the Meru (main bead) - turn the mālā and continue

**Afternoon Practice (12:00 - 2:00 PM):**
1. Brief worship
2. Complete afternoon japa quota (12 mālās)
3. Single vegetarian meal (if eating - see fasting rules)

**Evening Worship (6:00 - 9:00 PM):**
1. Sandhyā Vandana at sunset
2. Light lamp
3. Perform ārātī
4. Complete evening japa quota (minimum 12 mālās)
5. Shiva Stuti or Śiva Mahimna Stotra recitation

**Night Practice (Before sleep):**
1. Final japa session
2. Complete remaining quota if short
3. Meditation on Shiva
4. Sleep on floor or simple mat (not luxurious bed)

**Total Daily Japa Target:**
- 1,25,000 ÷ 11 = approximately 11,364 mantras per day
- This equals about 106 mālās per day
- Minimum 4 sessions of 25+ mālās each

### Fasting Rules (Upavāsa Niyama)

**Option 1: Complete Fast (Nirāhāra)**
- Only water during entire anusthan
- Most austere, maximum merit
- Only for those with strong constitution
- Not recommended without experienced guidance

**Option 2: Fruit Fast (Phala Āhāra)**
- Only fruits and milk
- One or two times per day
- Very effective balance

**Option 3: Single Meal (Ekabhukta)**
- One sattvic meal per day at noon
- No onion, garlic, meat, eggs
- No tamasic or rajasic foods
- This is the recommended option for householders

**General Dietary Rules:**
- No non-vegetarian food
- No alcohol or intoxicants
- No onion and garlic
- No stale or leftover food
- Cook food yourself or have a pure person cook
- Offer food to Shiva before eating
- Eat in silence
- Eat before sunset

### Behavioral Rules (Ācāra Niyama)

**Must Do:**
- Maintain Brahmacharya (celibacy)
- Speak truth only
- Practice non-violence
- Wake before sunrise
- Sleep after completing evening worship
- Maintain cleanliness
- Keep silence as much as possible (Mauna Vrata)
- Stay within home/ashram premises

**Must Avoid:**
- Sexual activity of any kind
- Anger, hatred, jealousy
- Gossip and idle talk
- Entertainment (TV, movies, social media)
- Sleeping during day
- Using leather items
- Cutting nails or hair
- Shaving
- Looking at own reflection (mirrors)
- Contact with women during their period
- Attending funerals or birth ceremonies

### Special Procedures

**First Day (Ārambha):**
- Take Sankalpa after morning bath
- Perform special elaborate puja
- Start japa from today

**Fifth or Sixth Day (Madhya):**
- Perform Rudrābhiṣeka with 11 recitations of Rudra
- Offer special naivedya
- This is the midpoint - renew commitment

**Last Day (Pūrṇāhuti/Samāpana):**
- Complete all remaining japa
- Perform Śata Rudrī or Ekādaśa Rudrī
- Mahābhiṣeka with all sacred substances
- Grand ārātī
- Homa (fire ceremony) if possible
- Feed Brahmins or poor people
- Offer Dakṣiṇā
- Take completion sankalpa

### Completion Procedure (Samāpana Vidhi)

On the 11th day:

1. Complete all japa before noon
2. Perform grand abhiṣeka
3. Special puja with elaborate offerings
4. Recite Shiva Sahasranāma or Rudra
5. Perform Havan if possible with:
   - Til (sesame)
   - Ghee
   - Samidhā (sacred wood)
   - 1/10th of total japa count as havan āhuti

6. Take final sankalpa:
"Yad-akṣara-pada-bhraṣṭaṃ mātrā-hīnaṃ ca yad-bhavet |
Tat sarvaṃ kṣamyatāṃ deva prasīda parameśvara ||"
(O Lord, please forgive any errors in pronunciation, counting, or procedure)

7. Offer donations:
   - Feed at least one Brahmin or poor person
   - Offer cloth, food items
   - Give Dakṣiṇā according to capacity

8. Receive blessings from Guru (if available)

9. Break fast with sattvic prasāda

### Specific Anusthans for Specific Purposes

**For Removing Obstacles:**
- Mantra: "Om Namaḥ Śivāya"
- Additional: Gaṇeśa mantra daily before Shiva japa
- Offer: Dhatura flowers

**For Health and Longevity:**
- Mantra: Mahāmṛtyuñjaya Mantra
- Abhiṣeka: With milk and honey especially
- Offer: Bilva and white flowers

**For Marriage:**
- Mantra: "Om Namaḥ Śivāya" or Kāmeśvara mantra
- Perform Pārvatī-Parameśvara puja together
- Offer: Red flowers

**For Spiritual Progress:**
- Mantra: "Om Namaḥ Śivāya" with dhyāna
- Extended meditation sessions
- Study Shiva Purāṇa

**For Wealth:**
- Mantra: Combined Shiva-Lakṣmī worship
- Include Kubera mantra
- Offer: Gold/yellow flowers

### If Rules Are Broken

If any rule is accidentally broken:

**Minor Breach:**
- Extra 1,000 mantras that day
- Prāyaścitta (atonement) mantra:
"Om Śivohaṃ Śivah Kevalohaṃ" (108 times)

**Major Breach:**
- Fast for one full day on water
- Extra 10,000 mantras
- Restart may be needed for serious violations

**If Anusthan Cannot Be Completed:**
- Perform partial completion puja
- Offer prayers for forgiveness
- Resolve to complete a fresh anusthan later
- The merit of what was done is not lost

### Signs of Successful Anusthan

- Dreams of Lord Shiva, Lingam, or sacred symbols
- Feeling of peace and lightness
- Natural reduction in food desire
- Increased clarity of mind
- Spontaneous tears of devotion
- Sense of Shiva's presence
- Resolution of the purpose (if specific purpose anusthan)

### Post-Anusthan Guidelines

After completing anusthan:

1. Continue regular Shiva worship daily
2. Maintain some level of dietary discipline
3. Gradually return to normal routine
4. Share merit with all beings:
"Sarve Bhavantu Sukhinaḥ | Sarve Santu Nirāmayāḥ |
Sarve Bhadrāṇi Paśyantu | Mā Kaścid-Duḥkha-Bhāg-Bhavet ||"

5. Observe monthly Pradoṣa and Śivarātri

---

## Key Points Summary

1. **Duration:** 11 days is traditional for Shiva Anusthan
2. **Japa Count:** 1,25,000 (1.25 lakh) Pañcākṣara mantras
3. **Daily Schedule:** 4 sessions of japa - morning, noon, evening, night
4. **Fasting:** At least one meal per day, sattvic vegetarian
5. **Celibacy:** Complete Brahmacharya is essential
6. **Silence:** Maintain mauna as much as possible
7. **Dedication:** Single-pointed focus on Shiva
8. **Completion:** Proper samāpana with havan and dāna

Remember the words of Lord Shiva: "Whichever devotee worships Me with steadfast devotion, following the prescribed rules, I destroy all their obstacles and grant them their heart's desires. But the devotee who worships Me without desiring anything - that one I keep close to My heart."

Om Namaḥ Śivāya
