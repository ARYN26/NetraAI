# Shiva Purana - Mode of Worshipping Shiva
Source: Vidyeśvarasaṃhitā (Shiva Purana)

## Chapter 11: Mode of Worshipping Śiva

### Daily Worship Procedure (Nitya Puja Vidhi)

The devotee should rise before dawn during Brahma Muhurta (4:00-6:00 AM). After attending to natural calls, one should perform Ācamana (sipping of water) three times while reciting:
- "Om Keśavāya Svāhā" (first sip)
- "Om Nārāyaṇāya Svāhā" (second sip)
- "Om Mādhavāya Svāhā" (third sip)

### Sandhyā Vandana (Twilight Prayers)

Morning Sandhyā should be performed facing East. The sacred Gāyatrī Mantra should be chanted 108 times:
"Om Bhūr Bhuvaḥ Svaḥ | Tat Savitur Vareṇyaṃ | Bhargo Devasya Dhīmahi | Dhiyo Yo Naḥ Pracodayāt"

For Shiva devotees, the Rudra Gāyatrī is prescribed:
"Om Tatpuruṣāya Vidmahe | Mahādevāya Dhīmahi | Tanno Rudraḥ Pracodayāt"

### Bathing Rituals (Snāna Vidhi)

Before worship, the devotee must bathe with sacred intention. While bathing, recite:
"Gaṅge Ca Yamune Caiva Godāvari Sarasvati | Narmade Sindhu Kāveri Jale'smin Sannidhiṃ Kuru"
(O Ganga, Yamuna, Godavari, Sarasvati, Narmada, Sindhu, and Kaveri - please be present in this water)

### Śiva Lingam Worship

The Lingam represents the formless aspect of Lord Shiva. Worship proceeds as follows:

1. **Dhyāna (Meditation)**: Meditate on the form of Shiva - adorned with serpents, holding the trident, with the crescent moon on his head, the third eye on his forehead, and the Ganga flowing from his matted locks.

2. **Āvāhana (Invocation)**: "Om Śivāya Namaḥ - Please be present in this Lingam, O Lord"

3. **Āsana (Seat offering)**: "Om Āsanārthaṃ Puṣpāṇi Samarpayāmi"

4. **Pādya (Water for feet)**: Offer water at the base: "Om Pādyaṃ Samarpayāmi"

5. **Arghya (Offering)**: "Om Arghyaṃ Samarpayāmi"

6. **Ācamanīya (Water to sip)**: "Om Ācamanīyaṃ Samarpayāmi"

7. **Snāna (Bathing)**: Bathe the Lingam with:
   - Pure water (Jala)
   - Milk (Dugdha)
   - Yogurt (Dadhi)
   - Honey (Madhu)
   - Ghee (Ghṛta)
   - Sugar water (Śarkarā)
   This is called Pañcāmṛta Abhiṣeka.

8. **Vastra (Cloth)**: Offer sacred cloth: "Om Vastrāṇi Samarpayāmi"

9. **Yajñopavīta (Sacred thread)**: "Om Yajñopavītaṃ Samarpayāmi"

10. **Gandha (Sandalwood paste)**: Apply sandalwood paste: "Om Gandhaṃ Samarpayāmi"

11. **Puṣpa (Flowers)**: Offer Bilva leaves (most sacred to Shiva) and flowers: "Om Puṣpāṇi Samarpayāmi"

12. **Dhūpa (Incense)**: "Om Dhūpaṃ Āghrāpayāmi"

13. **Dīpa (Lamp)**: Wave lit lamp: "Om Dīpaṃ Darśayāmi"

14. **Naivedya (Food offering)**: "Om Naivedyaṃ Samarpayāmi"

15. **Tāmbūla (Betel)**: "Om Tāmbūlaṃ Samarpayāmi"

16. **Dakṣiṇā (Offering)**: "Om Dakṣiṇāṃ Samarpayāmi"

17. **Pradakṣiṇā (Circumambulation)**: Walk around the Lingam clockwise three times

18. **Namaskāra (Prostration)**: Full prostration (Sāṣṭāṅga Praṇāma)

### Sacred Bilva Leaves

Lord Shiva said: "One who offers Me even a single Bilva leaf with devotion attains My abode. The three leaflets of Bilva represent My three eyes, the three Guṇas, and the Trimūrti."

The leaf should be offered with the stalk pointing towards oneself and the smooth side upwards:
"Tridalaṃ Triguṇākāraṃ Trinetraṃ Ca Triyāyudham | Trijanma Pāpa Saṃhāraṃ Eka Bilvaṃ Śivārpaṇam"

### Flowers Pleasing to Shiva

Different flowers yield different fruits:
- White flowers: Peace and purity
- Red flowers: Victory and power
- Blue flowers: Spiritual wisdom
- Dhatura flowers: Destruction of enemies and obstacles
- Ketaki (Pandanus): Should NOT be offered (cursed by Shiva)

### Rudra Abhiṣeka

For special occasions, the Rudra Abhiṣeka involves chanting the Śrī Rudram (Namakam and Chamakam) while performing abhiṣeka:

The Śrī Rudram begins:
"Namaḥ Śivāya Ca Śivatarāya Ca"

This should be recited eleven times (Ekādaśa Rudrī) for maximum benefit.

### Monday Worship (Somavāra Vrata)

Mondays are especially sacred to Lord Shiva. On this day:
- Fast until evening
- Perform abhiṣeka with milk
- Chant "Om Namaḥ Śivāya" 108 times minimum
- Offer Bilva leaves
- Listen to or recite Shiva stories

### Pradoṣa Vrata

The 13th tithi (day) of each lunar fortnight is Pradoṣa. Worship during the Pradoṣa kāla (1.5 hours before and after sunset) yields immense merit:
- Fasting from sunrise
- Evening worship with full sixteen upachāras
- Circumambulation of Shiva temple
- Chanting of Shiva Mahimna Stotram

### Mahāśivarātri

The greatest night of Shiva worship falls on the 14th day of the dark fortnight in Māgha/Phālguna month. The devotee should:
- Observe complete fast (nirāhāra vrata)
- Stay awake the entire night (jāgara)
- Perform abhiṣeka in each of the four praharas (quarters of night)
- Chant "Om Namaḥ Śivāya" continuously
- Listen to Shiva Purāṇa recitation

During each prahara, specific offerings are made:
- First prahara: Milk abhiṣeka
- Second prahara: Curd abhiṣeka
- Third prahara: Ghee abhiṣeka
- Fourth prahara: Honey abhiṣeka

### The Pañcākṣara Mantra

"Om Namaḥ Śivāya" - This five-syllabled mantra is the essence of all Shiva worship:
- Na = Earth element, removes sin
- Ma = Water element, gives devotion
- Śi = Fire element, grants liberation
- Vā = Air element, destroys ego
- Ya = Ether element, gives union with Shiva

Japa of this mantra should be done:
- Minimum: 108 times daily
- Regular practice: 1,008 times
- Intense practice: 10,008 times
- Purascharana: 5 lakh (500,000) times

### Chapter 14: Directions for Worship

Lord Shiva instructs:

"A devotee should worship Me with whatever means he has - even water offered with devotion pleases Me. I am not pleased by elaborate rituals performed without devotion, but even a simple offering made with love reaches Me.

The place of worship should be clean and peaceful. Face North or East while worshipping. The mind should be focused entirely on Me. External rituals are secondary to internal devotion.

A householder (gṛhastha) can worship according to his capacity. The sannyāsī should worship through meditation alone. The tantric practitioner follows additional procedures as instructed by the Guru.

I am present in all beings. One who sees Me in all and all in Me - that devotee is most dear to Me. Worship of the Guru, serving the devotees, and helping the poor are also forms of worshipping Me."

### Shiva Ārātrika

The final offering of waving lit lamps (ārātī) with the five-lamp holder (pañcārtika):

"Om Jaya Shiva Oṁkāra, Bhaja Shiva Oṁkāra
Brahmā Viṣṇu Sadāśiva Arddhāṅgī Dhārā"

After ārātī, take the blessed water (tīrtha), apply sacred ash (vibhūti) on the forehead, and receive prasāda.

### Vibhūti (Sacred Ash)

Sacred ash from the Shiva fire should be applied on the forehead in three horizontal lines (tripuṇḍra):
"Vibhūti represents the burning away of ignorance and ego. By wearing it, the devotee remembers the ultimate truth - that all physical forms return to ash."

Apply while chanting: "Tryambakam Yajāmahe Sugandhim Puṣṭivardhanam | Urvārukam Iva Bandhanān Mṛtyor Mukṣīya Māmṛtāt"

---

## Key Points for Daily Practice

1. Rise early (Brahma Muhurta)
2. Perform Ācamana and Sandhyā
3. Bathe with sacred intention
4. Worship Shiva Lingam or image with minimum 16 upachāras
5. Chant "Om Namaḥ Śivāya" at least 108 times
6. Offer Bilva leaves when available
7. Conclude with ārātī and prostration
8. Maintain purity of body, speech, and mind throughout the day

Remember: "Devotion (Bhakti) is greater than rituals (Karma). Knowledge (Jñāna) is greater than devotion. But the highest is the combination of all three - devoted service performed with wisdom."
